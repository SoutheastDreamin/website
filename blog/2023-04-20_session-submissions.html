2023 Session Submission & Selection

For our 2023 conference we recieved 162 session submissions! However, we only have room for 35 sessions so let's take a look at the breakdown of the submitted sessions, the speaker demographics and the sessions we selected.

Read our previous post about how Southeast Dreamin' does it's session selection

Session Submissions

In our return to Atlanta last year, we recieved 99 sessions submitted to be selected for 30 sessions. This year saw a 163% increase in submissions bringing our session submissions up to 162 which is the most submissions we have on record.

The breakdown of the session tracks roughly matches the percentages we have seen in the past. The majority of the sessions going to our Administrator and Developer tracks. Also, you can see a distinct uptick in session submissions days before the submission window closes.

Session Selection

We're not going to rehash our process for session selection here but let's take a look at the sessions we did select. As you can see, we end up choosing more sessions (by percentage) for the executive, business analysis, marketing, end user and non-profit tracks. So, if you want a better chance of having your session selected next year, we'd recommend branching out and submitting extra talks into a smaller pool of sessions.

Diversity and Inclusion

One of the biggest thing that we tried to be mindful of this year while making our session selection was to choose speakers that covered all of our vibrant community so that hopefully everyone attending could find someone that represented themselves amongst our speakers.

Looking ahead

Another reason we collected data about our speaker's demographics is to know where we need to expand our influence into under represented spaces and try to raise them up using our platform. Some groups that we can see we need to try to increase our engagement with is that of the Transgender, Agender / I don't identify with any gender and Indigenous, Native or Aboriginal communities. Reviewing all the submitted sessions, we did not have any submissions from these communities and we want to make sure that we are removing any barriers for them and encourging them to submit sessions
